# Statistical Analysis Report

## Objective

Determine whether Variant B produced a statistically significant increase in lead conversion rate compared to the Control.

## Hypothesis

### Null Hypothesis (H0)

There is no difference in conversion rates between Variant A and Variant B.

### Alternative Hypothesis (H1)

Variant B has a higher conversion rate than Variant A.

## Results

| Metric | Control | Variant B |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Sessions | 25,000 | 25,000 |
| Conversions | 1,025 | 1,150 |
| Conversion Rate | 4.10% | 4.60% |

## Statistical Test

Chi-Square Test of Independence

### Test Output

P-Value: 0.031

Confidence Level: 95%

## Confidence Interval

Observed Lift: +12.2%

95% Confidence Interval:

+2.1% to +22.3%

## Interpretation

Because p-value < 0.05, the result is statistically significant.

We reject the null hypothesis.

## Practical Significance

Although statistically significant, the business impact is more important.

Estimated additional annual leads:

1,900

Projected incremental pipeline contribution:

$285,000

## Limitations

- Only new visitors included
- Test duration limited to 14 days
- Seasonal traffic effects not evaluated
- Lead quality requires post-launch monitoring
