# Segment Insights

## Objective

Evaluate experiment performance across key audience segments to identify where the CTA change generated the strongest impact and to inform rollout decisions.

---

## Summary

Variant B outperformed the Control across all major segments analyzed. The largest gains were observed among mobile users and organic traffic visitors, suggesting that the revised CTA messaging resonated most strongly with users demonstrating higher intent but lower commitment.

---

## Device Performance

### Mobile

| Metric          | Control | Variant B |
| --------------- | ------- | --------- |
| Sessions        | 12,450  | 12,510    |
| Conversion Rate | 3.8%    | 4.4%      |
| Relative Lift   | -       | +15.8%    |

#### Insight

Mobile users responded most positively to the new CTA messaging.

The previous CTA may have appeared overly sales-oriented on smaller screens. The revised messaging reduced perceived commitment and increased form submissions.

#### Recommendation

Continue prioritizing mobile optimization initiatives and consider additional CTA experiments specifically targeting mobile visitors.

---

### Desktop

| Metric          | Control | Variant B |
| --------------- | ------- | --------- |
| Sessions        | 10,850  | 10,920    |
| Conversion Rate | 4.7%    | 5.1%      |
| Relative Lift   | -       | +8.5%     |

#### Insight

Desktop users also showed improvement, although the relative lift was smaller than mobile.

Desktop visitors generally demonstrated stronger baseline conversion rates, limiting the potential magnitude of improvement.

#### Recommendation

Retain Variant B for desktop traffic and evaluate additional landing page optimization opportunities.

---

## Traffic Source Performance

### Organic Search

| Metric          | Control | Variant B |
| --------------- | ------- | --------- |
| Conversion Rate | 4.0%    | 4.5%      |
| Relative Lift   | -       | +13.2%    |

#### Insight

Organic visitors generated substantial gains from the new CTA.

These users are often in research mode and may prefer lower-pressure messaging before engaging with sales.

#### Recommendation

Deploy Variant B across all organic landing experiences.

---

### Paid Search

| Metric          | Control | Variant B |
| --------------- | ------- | --------- |
| Conversion Rate | 4.6%    | 4.8%      |
| Relative Lift   | -       | +5.0%     |

#### Insight

Paid traffic showed modest improvement.

Users arriving from paid campaigns may already possess stronger purchase intent, reducing the impact of CTA wording changes.

#### Recommendation

Continue monitoring paid traffic performance and consider testing offer positioning rather than CTA wording.

---

## Geographic Analysis

### North America

Lift: +11.9%

Variant B performed consistently across the largest traffic segment and contributed the majority of incremental conversions.

### Europe

Lift: +9.1%

Performance remained positive but below the global average.

Further localization testing may improve results.

### APAC

Lift: +14.4%

Strong response observed despite lower traffic volume.

Additional experiments should be considered after rollout.

---

## Statistical Validation

Segment-level analysis was conducted only after overall experiment significance was achieved.

Observed improvements were directionally consistent across all major audience segments.

No segment demonstrated a statistically significant negative impact.

---

## Business Implications

Key findings indicate that:

1. Mobile visitors generated the highest relative improvement.
2. Organic traffic benefited most from revised messaging.
3. No segment experienced conversion decline.
4. Variant B can be safely deployed to all eligible traffic.
